Mashed Potatoes

This mashed potato recipe is perfect if you are looking for a good and old-fashioned mashed potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound baking potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled, or to taste (Optional)
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Steps to Prepare

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add potatoes and garlic, lower heat to medium, and simmer until potatoes are tender, 15 to 20 minutes.
  2. When the potatoes are almost finished, heat milk and butter in a small saucepan over low heat until butter is melted.
  3. Drain potatoes and return to the pot. Slowly add warm milk mixture, blending it in with a potato masher or electric mixer until potatoes are smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper.

Equipment

  • Large Pot
    1. Fill the pot with water.
    2. Bring the water to a boil.
  • Peeler and Knife
    1. Peel the potatoes
    2. Use the knife to cut the potatoes into quarters
  • Masher
    1. Once well-drained, mash the potatoes with a masher and add butter, milk, salt and pepper
    2. You can also use an electric mixer.
  • Mixing Bowl

Serving Suggestions

  1. You should have about 6 servings.
  2. Depending on how many people you are serving, you should use about 1 pound of potatoes for 2 people. If you have 4 people, use 2 pounds.
  3. One serving of mashed potatoes should be around 281 calories.
